üî¨ The Science of Reading

According to the National Reading Panel (2000) and recent neurological studies:

  • Predictor of Success: Phonemic awareness is the single best predictor of early reading success.
  • Neurological Wiring: Explicit instruction builds the neural pathways connecting the auditory and visual processing centers of the brain.
  • Prevention: Early intervention in K-1 can prevent reading difficulties in later grades.

üìà Progression of Skills

Instruction follows a hierarchy from simple (large units) to complex (smallest units).

Early Skills (Pre-K/Early K)

  • Rhyme: Recognizing words that sound the same at the end.
  • Alliteration: Recognizing words with the same starting sound.
  • Sentence Segmentation: Counting words in a sentence.

Basic Skills (Mid-Kindergarten)

  • Syllable Blending: "Ro" + "bot" = Robot.
  • Syllable Segmentation: Clapping parts of a word (nap-kin).
  • Onset-Rime: /c/ - /at/ = cat.

Advanced Skills (Late K/1st Grade)

  • Phoneme Blending: /d/ /o/ /g/ = dog.
  • Phoneme Segmentation: Telling the sounds in "ship" (/sh/ /i/ /p/).
  • Manipulation: "Say 'cat'. Change /c/ to /b/. (Bat)".
